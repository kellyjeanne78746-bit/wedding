The Venue
Villa Mondragone

A magnificent 16th-century villa nestled in the Alban Hills, offering breathtaking panoramic views across the Roman countryside. The perfect setting for an unforgettable celebration.

Via Frascati, 51
00078 Monte Porzio Catone, Roma, Italy
Discover the Villa View on Map

The ceremony and reception areas are on the ground floor with step-free access. An elevator connects the garden level to the upper terrace. Accessible parking spaces are reserved near the entrance with accessible restrooms on the main level.

Please let us know about any mobility or accessibility needs in the RSVP so we can ensure your comfort.

On-site parking is available for guests. Accessible parking spaces are reserved near the main entrance. We also recommend taxi or our complimentary shuttle service from partner hotels.

Villa Mondragone features stunning Renaissance gardens and grand indoor spaces, including a magnificent terrace with sweeping views across the Roman countryside for the reception.

Getting There
Travel & Accommodation

Rome is well-connected by two airports. We recommend flying into Fiumicino (FCO) for the best connections. Book accommodation early — late May is beautiful in Rome!

✈️ Getting to Rome

  • Fiumicino Airport (FCO) — Main international airport, ~35 min to city center by Leonardo Express train (€14 · ~$15 USD)
  • Ciampino Airport (CIA) — Budget airlines, ~40 min to center by bus (€6 · ~$7 USD)
  • Taxi from FCO — Fixed rate €50 · ~$55 USD to central Rome
  • EU visitors — High-speed rail connections from major European cities
  • Visa info — Italy is part of the Schengen Area. Check entry requirements for your country at the Italian consulate website

Villa Mondragone is in the Castelli Romani hills, near Frascati. Staying nearby means less travel on the wedding day and a chance to explore the beautiful hill towns.

Questions
Frequently Asked
What's the dress code? +
Black tie optional. Think elegant cocktail attire or formal wear. Ladies, be mindful of garden paths if wearing heels — wedges or block heels are a great choice! Gentlemen, a dark suit or tuxedo is perfect.
What will the weather be like in Rome in late May? +
Late May in Rome is lovely — expect highs around 24-28°C (75-82°F) during the day and pleasant evenings around 15-18°C (59-64°F). It's one of the best times to visit! A light wrap for the evening is still wise. Parts of the reception will be outdoors, but indoor spaces are available.
Do I need a visa to travel to Italy? +
Italy is in the Schengen Area. Citizens of the EU, US, UK, Canada, Australia, and many other countries can visit for up to 90 days without a visa. Please check with your country's Italian consulate or embassy for your specific requirements well in advance.
Is the venue wheelchair accessible? +
Yes! Villa Mondragone has wheelchair-accessible paths to the ceremony and reception areas, accessible restrooms, and reserved parking near the entrance. Please indicate any accessibility needs on the RSVP form so we can ensure everything is prepared for you.
Can you accommodate food allergies? +
Absolutely. Our chef is experienced with dietary restrictions including vegetarian, vegan, halal, kosher, gluten-free, and all common allergies. Please be as specific as possible on the RSVP form and we will work with the kitchen to ensure a wonderful meal for you.
How do I get to the venue on the wedding day? +
We will provide complimentary shuttle buses from central Rome to Villa Mondragone in Monte Porzio Catone (~35-40 min drive). Taxis are also available, though we strongly recommend the shuttle as the venue is outside the city. If you're driving, the venue has a parking area. Please select your transport preference in the RSVP form and we'll keep you updated on shuttle times.
Are children welcome? +
Yes, children are welcome! Please let us know in the RSVP form so we can arrange appropriate seating, a children's menu, and any other needs. If you'd prefer an adults-only evening, we can help recommend trusted local babysitting services.
What currency is used in Italy? +
Italy uses the Euro (€). Credit cards are widely accepted in Rome, but it's a good idea to carry some cash for smaller shops, tips, and market visits. ATMs are plentiful throughout the city.
Can we extend our trip and explore Italy? +
We'd love that! Rome itself has endless things to explore, and you're just a short train ride from Florence (1.5 hrs), Naples & Amalfi Coast (1 hr), and Tuscany. We're planning optional group activities the day before and after the wedding — stay tuned for details!
